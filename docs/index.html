DELTA Protocol

Don’t trust claims. Verify change.

The internet can prove ownership. DELTA proves change.

DELTA is an open, zero-token cryptographic Proof of Change protocol for binding declared digital changes to evidence, verification results, signatures, audit artifacts, publication proofs, trust context, and private evidence commitments.

What DELTA proves

DELTA proves cryptographic consistency between declared artifacts. A valid DELTA proof can show that a declared change, evidence hash, signature, verification result, bundle, or public commitment matches a specific record.

What DELTA does not prove

DELTA does not prove legal identity, signer authority, regulatory compliance, real-world truth, evidence completeness, institutional approval, or production readiness by itself. This boundary is deliberate. Cryptography can prove binding and consistency. It does not automatically prove law, authority, or truth.

Public technical review is open

DELTA is currently open for external technical review by security researchers, protocol reviewers, software supply-chain engineers, CI/CD maintainers, auditors, cryptography-minded developers, and early integrators.
